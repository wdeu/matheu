About MathEU

Pedagogy & Background

For teachers and parents

Where does MathEU come from?

MathEU is based on the mental math concept developed by Grundschule Op de Host in Horst (Schleswig-Holstein, Germany), which received the German School Award in 2023. The school created a structured system where children can systematically practice mental arithmetic through 16 progressive levels.

A former student of the school implemented this concept as a German-language open-source application. MathEU builds on this foundation and makes it available in multiple languages ‚Äì currently eight European languages.

The pedagogical concept

1. Self-directed learning

In Practice Mode, children can choose which operation and difficulty level they want to practice. There is no traditional right or wrong ‚Äì each problem receives encouraging feedback, and children can work at their own pace.

2. Structured progression (16 levels)

In Level Mode, problems follow the tier system from Grundschule Op de Host:

  • Levels build on each other (e.g., first addition within 10, then within 20)
  • Each level unlocks only after completing the previous one with at least 80% correct answers
  • Children experience small, achievable learning steps instead of overwhelming challenges

3. Mental math without distraction

The app offers a Mental Math Mode where problems are only read aloud without numbers appearing on screen. This mirrors traditional classroom mental arithmetic and encourages focus on mental calculation.

4. Positive feedback

Instead of error counting, MathEU works with encouraging responses. Four different feedback styles (encouraging, simple, playful, teacher-like) can be selected ‚Äì depending on what motivates the child.

What makes MathEU different?

Multilingualism as core feature

Unlike the original German-only version, MathEU supports eight languages: German, English, French, Portuguese, Spanish, Italian, Polish, and Ukrainian.

This selection is based on the most common first and second languages among elementary school children in Europe:

  • English is learned as a foreign language by 83% of all EU primary students ‚Äì by far the most important school language
  • German is the most common native language in the EU (19% of EU citizens)
  • French is spoken as a native language by 15% of EU citizens
  • Italian (13% of EU citizens) and Polish (9%, disproportionately represented among children due to young population structure)
  • Spanish (9% of EU, increasingly from Latin American immigration)
  • Ukrainian: Since 2022, a major factor ‚Äì around 700,000 Ukrainian children were enrolled in EU schools in 2023/24, most at primary level
  • Portuguese: For Portuguese and Brazilian families in Europe

Multilingualism makes MathEU particularly valuable for:

  • Children growing up bilingual
  • Classrooms with high migration backgrounds
  • Language support (combining math + foreign language)
  • International schools

Technical implementation for classroom use

  • No registration required: The app runs directly in the browser
  • Works offline: After initial loading, it functions without internet
  • No tracking: No data is collected or transmitted to servers
  • Free: No ads, no in-app purchases

Text-to-speech

The app uses the device's built-in speech synthesis (smartphone, tablet, computer) to read problems aloud. This works in all eight languages and can be individually adjusted ‚Äì helpful for children with reading difficulties or when learning languages.

  • Android: Settings ‚Üí Language & Input ‚Üí Text-to-Speech ‚Üí Install voice

Especially recommended when children practice in a language other than the system language.

Who is MathEU for?

Primary: Elementary school children (Grades 1-4)

The problems align with elementary curricula and cover the four basic operations up to 100. Through adjustable difficulty levels, the app can be used by both first-graders and advanced fourth-graders.

Multilingual families

Children growing up bilingual or learning a foreign language can practice problems in different languages in MathEU. This combines mathematics with language development.

Teachers

The app is suitable for:

  • Independent work phases
  • Differentiation (each child works at their level)
  • Substitute teaching
  • Support instruction

What MathEU is not

MathEU does not replace mathematics instruction. The app exclusively trains mental arithmetic ‚Äì an important but small part of mathematical learning. Topics like geometry, word problems, or mathematical problem-solving are deliberately not covered.

The app is also not a reward system with points, stars, or leaderboards. There are no gamification elements that could distract from actual learning.

Technical background

MathEU is an open-source web application developed with modern web technologies (React, Vite). The complete source code is available on GitHub. The app is developed as a volunteer project and can be used by anyone free of charge.

Multilingualism was implemented with the help of Claude (an AI assistant from Anthropic), with all translations reviewed by native-speaking educators.
